Best Hostels Near Hong Kong Disneyland

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong Disneyland is a dream destination for travelers of all ages. While the park itself is packed with enchantment, finding affordable accommodation nearby can be a challenge, especially for backpackers, solo travelers, or families on a tight budget. The good news? There are several hostels near Hong Kong Disneyland that offer comfort, convenience, and a touch of local flavor without breaking the bank. Whether you are looking for a place to crash after the fireworks or a base to explore the wider Lantau Island area, this guide will walk you through the best hostels, their unique perks, and how to make the most of your stay.

Why Choose a Hostel Near Hong Kong Disneyland?

Staying near Hong Kong Disneyland is not just about proximity to the park. The surrounding areas, particularly Tung Chung and Discovery Bay, offer a mix of nature, shopping, and cultural experiences. Hostels in these neighborhoods provide a social atmosphere where you can meet fellow travelers, swap tips, and even find travel buddies for park visits. Plus, with the MTR Disneyland Resort Line connecting directly to Sunny Bay and then to Tung Chung, getting to and from the park is a breeze.

The Cost Factor

Hotels on the Disneyland Resort property, such as the Hong Kong Disneyland Hotel or the Disney Explorers Lodge, can cost upwards of $200 per night. In contrast, hostels in the area often range from $30 to $80 per night, making them a smart choice for budget-conscious travelers. You save money on accommodation, which means more cash for souvenirs, dining, or upgrading to a Park Hopper ticket.

Location and Accessibility

Most hostels near Hong Kong Disneyland are located in Tung Chung, a town on Lantau Island that is just two MTR stops away from the Disneyland Resort station. Tung Chung is also the gateway to Ngong Ping 360, the Tian Tan Buddha, and the Po Lin Monastery, so you can combine your Disney trip with a cultural excursion. Some hostels are also in Discovery Bay, a quieter residential area with ferry access to Central Hong Kong, offering a more relaxed vibe.

Top Hostels Near Hong Kong Disneyland

Here is a curated list of the best hostels in the area, each with its own personality and set of amenities. I have included details about the atmosphere, cleanliness, and nearby attractions to help you make an informed choice.

1. Yesinn Hostel (Tung Chung Branch)

Yesinn is a well-known hostel chain in Hong Kong, and its Tung Chung branch is a favorite among Disneyland visitors. Located just a 5-minute walk from Tung Chung MTR station, this hostel offers both dormitory beds and private rooms. The decor is modern and minimalist, with bright colors that create a cheerful vibe.

What Makes It Stand Out

  • Proximity to Transportation: The hostel is right next to the MTR, so you can be at Disneyland in under 10 minutes. The bus terminal is also nearby, making it easy to explore other parts of Lantau Island.
  • Common Area: The communal lounge is spacious and equipped with a TV, board games, and a small library. It is a great place to unwind after a long day at the park.
  • Free Wi-Fi: The internet speed is reliable, which is essential for planning your itinerary or uploading photos to social media.

Nearby Attractions

  • Citygate Outlets: A massive shopping mall with discounted designer brands. It is a 10-minute walk from the hostel and perfect for picking up last-minute souvenirs.
  • Tung Chung Fort: A historical site from the Qing Dynasty, located about 15 minutes away by foot. It offers a glimpse into Hong Kong's past and is a nice contrast to the Disney magic.

Who Should Stay Here

Yesinn is ideal for solo travelers and small groups who value convenience and a social atmosphere. The staff are friendly and speak English, Cantonese, and Mandarin, so communication is easy.

2. Hop Inn at Tung Chung

Hop Inn is a boutique hostel that feels more like a cozy guesthouse. It is located in a residential building near the Tung Chung waterfront, offering stunning views of the sea and the distant mountains. The hostel has a limited number of rooms, which means it is quieter and more intimate than larger hostels.

What Makes It Stand Out

  • Scenic Views: Some rooms have balconies overlooking the harbor. Watching the sunrise with a cup of coffee is a memorable experience.
  • Home-Cooked Breakfast: The hostel offers a simple breakfast of toast, eggs, and fresh fruit for a small fee. It is a nice touch that saves you time and money in the morning.
  • Bicycle Rental: You can rent bicycles from the hostel to explore the Tung Chung promenade or ride to nearby beaches. It is a fun way to see the area.

Nearby Attractions

  • Tung Chung Bay: A peaceful walking path along the water, perfect for a morning jog or an evening stroll. You can see the Hong Kong–Zhuhai–Macau Bridge in the distance.
  • Ngong Ping 360: The cable car station is a 15-minute walk from the hostel. The ride to Ngong Ping Village offers breathtaking views of the mountains and the South China Sea.

Who Should Stay Here

Hop Inn is perfect for couples or solo travelers who want a more relaxed, homey environment. The limited capacity means you will not have to deal with noisy crowds.

3. Disneyland Backpackers Lodge (A Misnomer, But Worth Mentioning)

Despite its name, this is not an official Disney property. It is a small hostel located in a village near the Disneyland Resort, about a 20-minute walk from the park entrance. The lodge is run by a local family and offers a rustic experience that is hard to find elsewhere in Hong Kong.

What Makes It Stand Out

  • Village Atmosphere: The hostel is surrounded by traditional houses and small gardens. It feels like stepping back in time, away from the skyscrapers of Hong Kong Island.
  • Budget-Friendly Dorms: Dorm beds start at just $25 per night, making it one of the cheapest options in the area. Private rooms are also available for around $60.
  • Authentic Local Food: The family who runs the hostel sometimes cooks traditional Hakka dishes for guests. It is a great way to try homemade cuisine.

Nearby Attractions

  • Peng Chau Island: A small, car-free island reachable by ferry from Discovery Bay. It is a hidden gem with hiking trails and seafood restaurants.
  • Disneyland Resort: Obviously, the main attraction is just a short walk away. The hostel is close enough that you can return for a midday nap if you need a break from the crowds.

Who Should Stay Here

This hostel is best for adventurous travelers who want to experience local life. It is not as polished as other hostels, but the authenticity and warmth of the hosts make up for it.

4. Mini Hotel Central (A Slightly Farther Option)

Okay, this one is not exactly near Disneyland, but it deserves a mention because of its incredible value and easy access to the park. Mini Hotel Central is located in the heart of Hong Kong Island, near the Central MTR station. From there, you can take the Tung Chung Line to Sunny Bay and transfer to the Disneyland Resort Line. The total travel time is about 40 minutes, which is not bad considering the savings.

What Makes It Stand Out

  • Ultra-Low Prices: Dorm beds start at $20 per night, and private rooms are around $50. This is one of the cheapest options in all of Hong Kong.
  • Central Location: You are steps away from Lan Kwai Fong, SoHo, and the Mid-Levels Escalators. After a day at Disneyland, you can explore the city's nightlife.
  • Modern Facilities: The hostel is clean and well-maintained, with key card access and secure lockers. The bathrooms are shared but spotless.

Nearby Attractions

  • Victoria Peak: A short taxi ride or a 20-minute walk from the hostel. The view from the Peak is iconic, especially at sunset.
  • Star Ferry: The ferry terminal is a 15-minute walk away. A ride across Victoria Harbour costs less than $1 and offers stunning skyline views.

Who Should Stay Here

This is a great choice for travelers who want to combine Disneyland with exploring Hong Kong Island. You get the best of both worlds: a budget-friendly stay in the city center and easy access to the park.

Tips for Staying in Hostels Near Hong Kong Disneyland

To make your hostel experience as smooth as possible, here are some practical tips.

Book in Advance

Hong Kong Disneyland is a popular destination, especially during holidays and summer breaks. Hostels near the park fill up quickly, so book at least two weeks in advance. Use platforms like Hostelworld or Booking.com to compare prices and read reviews.

Pack Light

Hostel rooms are often small, and storage space is limited. Bring a backpack instead of a large suitcase, and pack only essentials. Most hostels provide towels and bedding, but it is a good idea to check in advance.

Use the MTR Smartly

The MTR is the fastest and most reliable way to get to Disneyland. Buy an Octopus card at any station for easy tap-in and tap-out. The card can also be used at convenience stores and some restaurants.

Respect Quiet Hours

Hostels are shared spaces, and other guests may be sleeping or resting. Keep noise to a minimum after 10 PM, especially in dormitories. Use headphones for music or videos.

Take Advantage of Hostel Amenities

Many hostels offer free luggage storage, which is useful if you have a late flight or want to explore after checking out. Some also have laundry facilities, so you can pack fewer clothes.

Beyond Disneyland: Exploring Lantau Island

Staying near Disneyland gives you the perfect opportunity to explore Lantau Island, which is full of natural and cultural attractions. Here are a few suggestions.

Ngong Ping Village and the Tian Tan Buddha

Take the Ngong Ping 360 cable car from Tung Chung to Ngong Ping Village. The ride lasts about 25 minutes and offers panoramic views of the island. Once there, visit the Tian Tan Buddha, a 34-meter-tall bronze statue that is one of Hong Kong's most iconic landmarks. The Po Lin Monastery, located nearby, serves vegetarian meals that are worth trying.

Tai O Fishing Village

Tai O is a traditional fishing village on the western side of Lantau Island. It is famous for its stilt houses, dried seafood, and boat tours that let you see the local wildlife, including pink dolphins. You can take a bus from Tung Chung to Tai O, and the journey takes about 30 minutes.

Discovery Bay

Discovery Bay is a residential area with a Mediterranean feel. It has a beach, a golf course, and several restaurants. You can take a ferry from Discovery Bay to Central Hong Kong, which is a scenic way to travel. Some hostels, like the Disneyland Backpackers Lodge, are located near Discovery Bay, making it easy to explore.

Hiking Trails

Lantau Island has several hiking trails, including the Lantau Trail, which stretches for 70 kilometers. For a shorter hike, try the trail to the Sunset Peak, which offers stunning views of the coastline. The trailhead is near Tung Chung, and the hike takes about 2-3 hours round trip.

The Social Aspect of Hostel Life

One of the best things about staying in a hostel is the opportunity to meet people from all over the world. Near Hong Kong Disneyland, you will find a mix of travelers, from families with young children to solo backpackers. Many hostels organize group activities, such as movie nights, city tours, or cooking classes. Participating in these events can enhance your trip and create lasting memories.

Language and Communication

English is widely spoken in Hong Kong, especially in tourist areas. Hostel staff are usually fluent in English, so you will not have trouble communicating. However, learning a few Cantonese phrases, like "nei hou" (hello) or "m goi" (thank you), can go a long way in building rapport with locals.

Final Thoughts on Hostel Stays Near Hong Kong Disneyland

Choosing a hostel near Hong Kong Disneyland is a smart way to stretch your travel budget while still enjoying a magical vacation. Whether you opt for the convenience of Yesinn, the charm of Hop Inn, the authenticity of the Disneyland Backpackers Lodge, or the city-center affordability of Mini Hotel Central, you will find a place that suits your needs. Remember to book early, pack light, and take advantage of the MTR for easy park access. And do not forget to explore Lantau Island beyond the theme park—there is so much to see and do.

Your adventure awaits. Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned Disney fan, the hostels near Hong Kong Disneyland will provide a comfortable and affordable base for your journey. So pack your bags, grab your Mickey ears, and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
