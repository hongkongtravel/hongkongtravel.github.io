A Complete Guide to Hong Kong’s Public Transport with Kids

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a bustling metropolis known for its efficient and extensive public transport system. For families traveling with kids, navigating the city’s transport options can be both exciting and overwhelming. From the iconic Star Ferry to the high-speed MTR, Hong Kong offers a variety of kid-friendly ways to explore. This guide covers everything you need to know about using public transport in Hong Kong with children, including tips, must-try rides, and hidden gems along the way.

Why Hong Kong’s Public Transport is Perfect for Families

Hong Kong’s public transport is not just convenient—it’s an adventure in itself. Kids will love the double-decker trams, the scenic Peak Tram, and the colorful minibuses zipping through the streets. The system is also incredibly safe, clean, and well-connected, making it ideal for families. Plus, many modes of transport offer discounted or free fares for children.

Key Benefits:

  • Affordability: Children under 12 often travel for half-price or free.
  • Efficiency: Trains and buses run frequently, reducing wait times.
  • Fun Factor: Unique rides like the Ngong Ping 360 cable car make transit part of the experience.

Getting Around: The Best Transport Options for Kids

1. MTR (Mass Transit Railway)

The MTR is Hong Kong’s backbone, connecting major districts with speed and ease. For families, it’s a stress-free way to travel.

Tips for Riding the MTR with Kids:

  • Use the Octopus Card: Kids aged 3–11 can get a Child Octopus Card for discounted fares.
  • Avoid Rush Hour: MTR trains get crowded during peak times (8–9:30 AM and 5–7 PM).
  • Look for Family-Friendly Stations: Stations like Disneyland Resort and Ocean Park have themed designs that kids love.

Must-Visit Stops:

  • Disneyland Resort Station: The Mickey Mouse-themed windows and vintage-style trains set the mood for magic.
  • Ocean Park Station: Bright, ocean-themed decor gets kids excited before they even enter the park.

2. Buses and Double-Decker Trams

Hong Kong’s buses and trams offer a scenic way to see the city. Kids will especially enjoy sitting on the top deck of a double-decker bus or tram.

Best Routes for Sightseeing:

  • Bus #15: Winds up to Victoria Peak with stunning views.
  • Tram #6: A historic ride through Central and Wan Chai.

Pro Tip:

  • Front-Seat Thrills: Let your child sit at the front of the upper deck for the best views.

3. Star Ferry

No trip to Hong Kong is complete without a ride on the Star Ferry. Crossing Victoria Harbour between Central and Tsim Sha Tsui is a quintessential experience.

Why Kids Love It:

  • Short and Sweet: The 10-minute ride is perfect for short attention spans.
  • Spectacular Views: The skyline, especially at night, is mesmerizing.

Best Time to Go:

  • Sunset Cruises: The harbor lights up beautifully as the sun goes down.

4. Peak Tram

The Peak Tram is one of the world’s steepest funicular railways, offering breathtaking views as it climbs to Victoria Peak.

Kid-Friendly Perks:

  • Interactive Displays: The Peak Tower has a "Sky Terrace 428" with telescopes and photo ops.
  • Madame Tussauds: A fun stop at the top for celebrity-loving kids.

5. Ngong Ping 360 Cable Car

For an unforgettable experience, take the cable car to Ngong Ping Village on Lantau Island. The 25-minute ride offers panoramic views of the mountains and sea.

Family Tips:

  • Crystal Cabin Upgrade: The glass-bottom cabins add extra excitement (best for brave kids!).
  • Visit the Big Buddha: The Tian Tan Buddha statue is a short walk from the cable car station.

Practical Tips for Smooth Travel with Kids

1. Stroller Accessibility

  • MTR: Most stations have elevators, but some older ones may require folding strollers.
  • Buses and Trams: Not all are stroller-friendly—baby carriers can be easier.

2. Snacks and Hydration

  • Pack light snacks and water, especially for longer rides like the Ngong Ping 360.

3. Rest Stops

  • Major MTR stations and shopping malls have clean, family-friendly restrooms.

4. Weather Preparedness

  • Hong Kong can be hot and humid—bring hats, sunscreen, and handheld fans for outdoor waits.

Hidden Gems: Fun Transport-Themed Activities

1. Hong Kong Tramways Museum

A small but charming museum in Sha Tin where kids can learn about the history of trams.

2. MTR Rides to Lesser-Known Stops

  • Tai Wai Station: Visit the nearby Hong Kong Heritage Museum.
  • Sai Kung: Take a minibus from Choi Hung Station for a seaside escape.

3. Aqua Luna Junk Boat

For a special treat, book a ride on a traditional red-sail junk boat for a harbor tour.

Final Thoughts

Hong Kong’s public transport isn’t just a way to get around—it’s part of the adventure. Whether your kids are train enthusiasts or just love a good view, the city’s diverse transit options will keep them entertained. With a little planning, your family can enjoy seamless, fun-filled travels across one of the world’s most dynamic cities.
