Hong Kong’s Best Scenic Spots for Group Picnics

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a vibrant city known for its towering skyscrapers, bustling markets, and rich cultural heritage. But beyond the urban chaos lies a treasure trove of scenic spots perfect for group picnics. Whether you're planning a family outing, a friends' gathering, or a corporate team-building event, these picturesque locations offer stunning views, fresh air, and plenty of space to relax and enjoy good food.

Victoria Peak: A Sky-High Picnic with a View

Why Victoria Peak?

Victoria Peak is one of Hong Kong’s most iconic destinations, offering panoramic views of the city skyline and Victoria Harbour. While many visitors come for the sightseeing, the Peak also boasts lush green spaces ideal for a picnic.

Best Picnic Spots at the Peak

  • Lugard Road Lookout: A quiet spot with benches and open grassy areas.
  • Peak Garden: A beautifully landscaped park with shaded areas and plenty of room for groups.

Pro Tips

  • Arrive early to secure a good spot, especially on weekends.
  • Bring a blanket and portable chairs for comfort.
  • Pack light snacks and drinks—there are limited food options nearby.

Repulse Bay: Sun, Sand, and Picnic Fun

Why Repulse Bay?

This popular beach is not just for swimming—it’s also a fantastic place for a seaside picnic. The soft sand, gentle waves, and nearby amenities make it a top choice for groups.

Best Picnic Spots at Repulse Bay

  • The Beachfront Lawn: A grassy area perfect for spreading out a picnic blanket.
  • The Promenade: Offers shaded seating with ocean views.

Pro Tips

  • Bring a cooler to keep drinks and food fresh.
  • Don’t forget sunscreen and umbrellas for shade.
  • Visit on weekdays to avoid crowds.

Tai Tam Country Park: A Nature Lover’s Paradise

Why Tai Tam Country Park?

For those who prefer a more rustic setting, Tai Tam Country Park is a haven of tranquility. With reservoirs, hiking trails, and abundant greenery, it’s perfect for a peaceful group picnic.

Best Picnic Spots in the Park

  • Tai Tam Reservoir: Scenic views with picnic tables nearby.
  • Dam Area: A spacious spot with plenty of shade.

Pro Tips

  • Wear comfortable shoes for light hiking.
  • Pack insect repellent—nature comes with bugs!
  • Bring reusable utensils to minimize waste.

Nan Lian Garden: A Serene Escape in the City

Why Nan Lian Garden?

This classical Chinese garden offers a peaceful retreat from the city’s hustle and bustle. The meticulously designed landscapes and tranquil ponds create a perfect backdrop for a relaxing picnic.

Best Picnic Spots in the Garden

  • The Pavilion Area: Shaded and quiet, ideal for small groups.
  • The Lotus Pond: A picturesque spot with benches.

Pro Tips

  • Respect the garden’s rules—no loud music or littering.
  • Visit during weekdays for a more private experience.
  • Try the vegetarian restaurant inside the garden for a post-picnic treat.

Lamma Island: A Quirky Island Picnic Adventure

Why Lamma Island?

Just a short ferry ride from Central, Lamma Island offers a laid-back vibe with stunning coastal views. It’s a great spot for groups looking to combine a picnic with a bit of exploration.

Best Picnic Spots on Lamma Island

  • Hung Shing Yeh Beach: A sandy beach with picnic-friendly areas.
  • Family Trail: Scenic spots along the hiking path with ocean views.

Pro Tips

  • Pack light—you’ll need to carry everything from the ferry.
  • Try local seafood at one of the island’s restaurants afterward.
  • Check ferry schedules in advance to avoid long waits.

Lion Rock Country Park: A Picnic with a Side of Adventure

Why Lion Rock Country Park?

For groups that love a challenge, Lion Rock offers breathtaking views after a rewarding hike. The park’s open spaces are perfect for a post-hike picnic.

Best Picnic Spots in the Park

  • Lion Rock Pavilion: A shaded area with benches.
  • MacLehose Trail Section: Open grassy spots along the trail.

Pro Tips

  • Start early to avoid the midday heat.
  • Bring plenty of water and energy snacks.
  • Wear sturdy shoes—the terrain can be uneven.

Sai Kung East Country Park: A Remote Picnic Haven

Why Sai Kung East Country Park?

If your group enjoys off-the-beaten-path adventures, this park is a hidden gem. With pristine beaches and rugged landscapes, it’s ideal for a secluded picnic.

Best Picnic Spots in the Park

  • Long Ke Wan Beach: A remote beach with crystal-clear water.
  • High Island Reservoir: Scenic spots with picnic tables.

Pro Tips

  • Plan transportation in advance—some areas are only accessible by boat or taxi.
  • Pack everything you need—there are no shops nearby.
  • Leave no trace to preserve the natural beauty.

Tips for a Perfect Group Picnic in Hong Kong

Packing Essentials

  • Lightweight picnic blanket or foldable chairs
  • Reusable utensils and containers
  • Cooler with ice packs
  • Trash bags for easy cleanup

Food Ideas

  • Finger foods like sandwiches, fruit, and cheese platters
  • Local snacks from Hong Kong bakeries
  • Refreshing drinks (think bubble tea or fresh coconut water)

Safety Reminders

  • Check weather forecasts before heading out.
  • Stay hydrated, especially in hot weather.
  • Be mindful of wildlife in country parks.

Hong Kong’s diverse landscapes offer endless possibilities for memorable group picnics. Whether you prefer beachside relaxation, mountain vistas, or urban gardens, there’s a perfect spot waiting for you and your friends or family. So grab your picnic basket, gather your group, and enjoy the great outdoors!
