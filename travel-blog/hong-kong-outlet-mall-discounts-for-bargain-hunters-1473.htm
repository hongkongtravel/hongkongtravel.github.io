Hong Kong Outlet Mall Discounts for Bargain Hunters

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a shopper’s paradise, and for those who love a good deal, the city’s outlet malls are a dream come true. From luxury brands at slashed prices to trendy streetwear steals, Hong Kong’s outlet scene offers something for every bargain hunter. Whether you're a tourist looking to stretch your travel budget or a local hunting for hidden gems, these shopping havens are worth exploring.

Why Hong Kong Outlet Malls Are a Must-Visit

Outlet malls in Hong Kong are more than just places to snag discounts—they’re destinations in themselves. With a mix of international and local brands, these malls cater to fashionistas, tech lovers, and home decor enthusiasts alike. The best part? Many of these outlets are located near major tourist spots, making them convenient stops during your Hong Kong adventure.

The Best Outlet Malls in Hong Kong

1. Citygate Outlets (Tung Chung)

Located near Ngong Ping 360 and Hong Kong International Airport, Citygate Outlets is one of the largest outlet malls in the city. Here, you’ll find over 80 brands offering discounts of up to 90% year-round. From Adidas and Nike to Coach and Kate Spade, the deals are unbeatable.

Pro Tip: Visit on weekdays to avoid crowds and enjoy a more relaxed shopping experience.

2. Horizon Plaza (Ap Lei Chau)

A hidden gem for luxury shoppers, Horizon Plaza houses high-end brands like Armani, Lane Crawford, and Ralph Lauren. Spread across 28 floors, this outlet mall is a treasure trove for those willing to hunt for designer pieces at a fraction of retail prices.

Don’t Miss: The homeware and furniture sections, where you can find stylish decor at outlet prices.

3. New Town Plaza (Sha Tin)

While not a traditional outlet mall, New Town Plaza frequently hosts seasonal sales and pop-up discount events. Brands like Zara, Uniqlo, and H&M often slash prices here, making it a hotspot for budget-conscious shoppers.

Bonus: The mall is connected to Sha Tin MTR station, so it’s super easy to access.

4. Dongmen Clothing Wholesale Market (Shenzhen, but Worth the Trip)

Okay, this one’s technically not in Hong Kong, but it’s just a short train ride away. If you’re up for a day trip, Shenzhen’s Dongmen Market offers dirt-cheap fashion, accessories, and souvenirs. Bargaining is expected, so sharpen your haggling skills!

Insider Tips for Scoring the Best Deals

  1. Timing Is Everything

    • Visit during major sale seasons like Chinese New Year, summer (June-August), and Christmas for extra discounts.
    • Many outlets restock on weekdays, so shopping early in the week can give you first dibs on new arrivals.

  2. Sign Up for Loyalty Programs

    • Some outlet malls offer additional discounts for members. For example, Citygate Outlets has a VIP club with exclusive promotions.

  3. Check for Combo Deals

    • Look for stores offering “buy one, get one free” or additional percentage-off discounts when you purchase multiple items.

  4. Bring Cash (Sometimes It Helps)

    • While most stores accept cards, smaller vendors or markets might offer better prices for cash payments.

Beyond Shopping: Food and Entertainment

Outlet malls in Hong Kong aren’t just about shopping—many have great dining options and entertainment facilities. For example:

  • Citygate Outlets has a food court with affordable local and international cuisine.
  • Horizon Plaza is near the scenic Ap Lei Chau waterfront, perfect for a post-shopping stroll.
  • New Town Plaza features an ice-skating rink and cinema for a full day of fun.

Final Thoughts

Hong Kong’s outlet malls are a bargain hunter’s dream, blending unbeatable discounts with convenience and variety. Whether you’re hunting for designer labels or everyday steals, these shopping spots deliver. So pack your bags, grab your wallet, and get ready to shop till you drop—Hong Kong style!
