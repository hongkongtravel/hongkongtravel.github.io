Hong Kong Hiking Trails with Free or Discounted Access

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is often celebrated for its dazzling skyline and bustling urban life, but beyond the concrete jungle lies a hiker’s paradise. With over 70% of its land designated as countryside, the city offers an impressive network of trails that cater to all skill levels. Best of all, many of these trails are free to access or offer discounted entry for certain groups. Whether you’re a local looking for a weekend escape or a traveler seeking an affordable adventure, here’s your guide to Hong Kong’s best hiking trails that won’t break the bank.

Top Free Hiking Trails in Hong Kong

Dragon’s Back Trail

Difficulty: Moderate
Duration: 2.5–3 hours
Why It’s Great: Voted one of the best urban hikes in Asia, Dragon’s Back offers panoramic views of Shek O Beach, Tai Long Wan, and the South China Sea. The trail is entirely free and easily accessible via public transport.

How to Get There: Take the MTR to Shau Kei Wan Station, then hop on Bus 9 to To Tei Wan. The trailhead is just a short walk from the bus stop.

Victoria Peak Circle Walk

Difficulty: Easy
Duration: 1–1.5 hours
Why It’s Great: This leisurely loop around Victoria Peak provides stunning views of Hong Kong’s skyline and harbor. While the Peak Tram isn’t free, you can hike up from Central or take a bus to avoid the cost.

Pro Tip: Visit at sunset for an unforgettable vista of the city lights coming to life.

Lantau Trail (Section 3: Pak Kung Au to Ngong Ping)

Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
Duration: 3–4 hours
Why It’s Great: This section leads to the iconic Tian Tan Buddha (Big Buddha) and the Wisdom Path. While the Ngong Ping 360 cable car is pricey, hiking up is free and rewards you with lush landscapes and cultural landmarks.

How to Get There: Take the MTR to Tung Chung Station, then catch Bus 11 or 23 to Pak Kung Au.

Discounted Hiking Access for Specific Groups

Country Parks & Special Offers

Hong Kong’s Country Parks are free to enter, but some attractions within them offer discounts:

  • Kadoorie Farm and Botanic Garden: Offers free entry on Wednesdays (usually HK$30).
  • Hong Kong Wetland Park: Seniors and children enjoy discounted tickets (HK$15–30).

Student Discounts: Many private trails or guided tours offer student discounts—always carry your ID.

Hidden Gems: Lesser-Known Free Trails

Pineapple Dam Nature Trail

Difficulty: Easy
Duration: 1 hour
Why It’s Great: A shaded, family-friendly trail in Shing Mun Country Park with WWII relics and monkeys along the way.

Tai Tam Reservoir Walk

Difficulty: Easy
Duration: 2 hours
Why It’s Great: A historic trail passing colonial-era dams and tranquil reservoirs. Perfect for history buffs and nature lovers alike.

Seasonal Highlights & Events

Autumn Hiking Festivals

Every October, NGOs and outdoor groups organize free guided hikes, such as the "HK Hiking Festival," with routes ranging from beginner to advanced.

Winter Sunrise Hikes

Trails like Sunset Peak and Tai Mo Shan become popular for sunrise views. While access is free, some groups offer discounted shuttle services.

Budget-Friendly Tips for Hikers

  1. Public Transport Over Taxis: Use buses or minibuses to reach trailheads—it’s cheaper and often faster.
  2. Pack Your Own Food: Avoid overpriced snacks at tourist spots.
  3. Join Free Walking Tours: Organizations like Walk in Hong Kong occasionally offer free hiking tours.

Eco-Friendly Hiking Practices

  • Stick to marked trails to protect fragile ecosystems.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle—many trails have refill stations.
  • Participate in volunteer-led clean-up hikes for a rewarding experience.

Hong Kong’s trails are a testament to the city’s natural beauty and cultural diversity. Whether you’re scaling Dragon’s Back or strolling through Tai Tam, these free and discounted hikes prove that adventure doesn’t have to come with a hefty price tag.
