Family-Friendly Hikes in Hong Kong with Stunning Views

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is often celebrated for its dazzling skyline and bustling streets, but beyond the urban jungle lies a treasure trove of natural beauty waiting to be explored. For families looking to escape the city’s hustle and bond over breathtaking landscapes, Hong Kong offers an array of family-friendly hikes that cater to all ages and fitness levels. These trails not only provide stunning views but also opportunities to connect with nature, spot wildlife, and create unforgettable memories.

Why Choose Family-Friendly Hikes in Hong Kong?

Hong Kong’s diverse terrain includes lush mountains, serene reservoirs, and coastal paths—many of which are easily accessible and well-maintained. Family-friendly hikes are typically shorter in distance, have gentle gradients, and feature amenities like rest stops, picnic areas, and clear signage. Plus, the views are nothing short of spectacular, from panoramic cityscapes to tranquil beaches.

Here are some of the best family-friendly hikes in Hong Kong that promise stunning scenery and an enjoyable experience for all.

1. Dragon’s Back Trail

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Duration: 2–3 Hours

One of Hong Kong’s most famous hikes, the Dragon’s Back Trail, is perfect for families seeking a mix of adventure and awe-inspiring views. Named for its undulating ridge that resembles a dragon’s spine, this trail offers sweeping vistas of Shek O Beach, Tai Long Wan, and the South China Sea.

The hike starts near To Tei Wan Village and ascends gently, making it manageable for kids. Along the way, you’ll pass through shaded forests and open ridges, with plenty of photo opportunities. The trail ends at Big Wave Bay, where families can relax on the beach or enjoy a refreshing swim.

Pro Tip: Bring plenty of water and sunscreen, as some sections are exposed to the sun.

2. Lamma Island Family Walk

Difficulty: Easy

Duration: 1.5–2 Hours

For a laid-back hike with coastal charm, head to Lamma Island. The family-friendly trail connects Yung Shue Wan and Sok Kwu Wan, two of the island’s main villages. The path is mostly flat, with minimal elevation gain, making it ideal for young children.

Highlights include scenic fishing villages, hidden beaches, and lush greenery. Stop by Hung Shing Yeh Beach for a picnic or explore the quirky shops and seafood restaurants in Sok Kwu Wan. The trail also offers glimpses of traditional Lamma Island life, from fishing boats to local temples.

Pro Tip: Take the ferry from Central Pier 4 to Yung Shue Wan and return from Sok Kwu Wan for a seamless loop.

3. Tai Tam Reservoir Hike

Difficulty: Easy

Duration: 1–2 Hours

Nestled in the heart of Hong Kong Island, the Tai Tam Reservoir hike is a serene escape with historical flair. The trail winds around the picturesque Tai Tam Tuk Reservoir, passing century-old dams and bridges built during the colonial era.

The flat, well-paved path is stroller-friendly, and the shaded sections provide relief from the sun. Kids will love spotting fish in the reservoir and exploring the quaint stone structures. For a longer adventure, extend the hike to Tai Tam Country Park, where you’ll find barbecue pits and lush picnic spots.

Pro Tip: Combine the hike with a visit to nearby Stanley Market for a full day of family fun.

4. Lion Rock Country Park – MacLehose Trail Section

Difficulty: Moderate

Duration: 2–3 Hours

For families with older kids or teens, the Lion Rock Country Park offers a slightly more challenging but rewarding hike. The MacLehose Trail section leading to Lion Rock Pavilion provides panoramic views of Kowloon and Hong Kong Island.

The trail is well-marked and includes stairs and gentle slopes. Once at the pavilion, you’ll be treated to one of the best viewpoints in Hong Kong—perfect for a family photo with the iconic skyline in the background.

Pro Tip: Start early to avoid the midday heat and bring snacks to enjoy at the viewpoint.

5. Tung Chung to Ngong Ping (via Wisdom Path)

Difficulty: Moderate

Duration: 3–4 Hours

For a cultural and scenic hike, the trail from Tung Chung to Ngong Ping is a fantastic choice. The path leads to the famous Wisdom Path, where giant wooden pillars inscribed with Buddhist prayers stand against a mountainous backdrop.

The hike includes a mix of paved paths and natural trails, with stunning views of Lantau Island and the South China Sea. Families can extend the adventure by visiting the Tian Tan Buddha (Big Buddha) and Po Lin Monastery at the end of the trail.

Pro Tip: Take the Ngong Ping 360 cable car back to Tung Chung for a thrilling end to the day.

Essential Tips for Family Hiking in Hong Kong

  • Pack Light but Smart: Bring water, snacks, hats, and sunscreen. A small first-aid kit is also handy.
  • Check the Weather: Avoid hiking during extreme heat or rain, as trails can become slippery.
  • Start Early: Mornings are cooler and less crowded.
  • Use Public Transport: Most trailheads are accessible by bus, ferry, or MTR.
  • Leave No Trace: Teach kids the importance of preserving nature by carrying out all trash.

Hong Kong’s family-friendly hikes are a fantastic way to explore the city’s natural side while enjoying quality time together. Whether you’re chasing coastal views, historical landmarks, or mountain panoramas, these trails offer something for everyone. So lace up your hiking shoes, grab the kids, and embark on an adventure you won’t forget!
