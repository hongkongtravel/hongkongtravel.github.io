The Best Dim Sum for a Family Reunion in Hong Kong

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a city that thrives on tradition, and few things embody this better than dim sum. For families planning a reunion, there’s no better way to bond than over steaming baskets of har gow, siu mai, and char siu bao. The ritual of sharing small plates, sipping tea, and chatting over a lazy brunch is a cherished experience. But with so many options, where should you go for the ultimate family-friendly dim sum feast? Here’s a curated list of the best spots in Hong Kong to enjoy this culinary tradition.

Top Dim Sum Restaurants for Large Groups

1. Tim Ho Wan (The World’s Cheapest Michelin-Starred Restaurant)

If your family loves great food without breaking the bank, Tim Ho Wan is a must-visit. Known for its crispy-bottomed baked BBQ pork buns, this Michelin-starred spot offers affordable yet exceptional dim sum. The open kitchen lets kids watch chefs at work, adding a fun element to the meal.

Must-Order Dishes:
- Baked BBQ pork buns
- Steamed egg cake
- Pan-fried turnip cake

2. Lung King Heen (Four Seasons Hong Kong)

For a more upscale reunion, Lung King Heen—the first Chinese restaurant to earn three Michelin stars—delivers an unforgettable experience. The harbor views and impeccable service make it ideal for special family gatherings.

Must-Order Dishes:
- Lobster and scallop dumplings
- Crispy roasted pork belly
- Baked abalone puff

3. Maxim’s Palace (City Hall)

A classic Hong Kong institution, Maxim’s Palace retains the old-world charm of pushcart dim sum. The grand ballroom setting and lively atmosphere make it perfect for big families who want a nostalgic experience.

Must-Order Dishes:
- Shrimp dumplings (har gow)
- Sticky rice in lotus leaf
- Egg tarts

Hidden Gems for a Local Experience

1. Lin Heung Tea House

This century-old teahouse is a no-frills spot where families can experience dim sum the way locals do. Be prepared to share tables and fight for the freshest baskets—it’s all part of the fun!

Must-Order Dishes:
- Pork and shrimp siu mai
- Chicken feet in black bean sauce
- Pineapple buns

2. Yum Cha (Modern Twist on Dim Sum)

If your family enjoys playful presentations, Yum Cha’s creative dim sum—like smiling custard buns and BBQ piggy buns—will be a hit. The Instagram-worthy dishes add a lighthearted touch to the meal.

Must-Order Dishes:
- Custard lava buns
- Truffle siu mai
- Matcha dumplings

Tips for a Smooth Dim Sum Family Reunion

1. Go Early or Make Reservations

Popular spots like Tim Ho Wan and Maxim’s Palace get crowded, especially on weekends. Arrive before noon or book ahead to avoid long waits.

2. Order a Variety of Dishes

Dim sum is about sharing, so mix steamed, fried, and baked items to cater to different tastes. Don’t forget dessert—egg tarts and mango pudding are crowd-pleasers!

3. Embrace the Tea Ritual

Tea is central to dim sum. Let the kids try pu-erh or jasmine tea, and teach them the custom of tapping the table to say "thank you" when someone refills their cup.

4. Explore Beyond the Restaurant

After your meal, take a family stroll through nearby markets like Temple Street Night Market or Stanley Market to soak in more local culture.

Final Thoughts

Whether you opt for a Michelin-starred feast or a bustling local teahouse, dim sum in Hong Kong is more than just a meal—it’s a celebration of family, tradition, and delicious food. So gather your loved ones, raise your teacups, and enjoy the flavors that make Hong Kong’s dim sum scene truly special.
