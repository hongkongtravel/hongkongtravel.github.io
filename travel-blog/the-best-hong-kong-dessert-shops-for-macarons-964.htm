The Best Hong Kong Dessert Shops for Macarons

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a paradise for food lovers, and its dessert scene is no exception. Among the countless sweet treats available, macarons hold a special place. These delicate, colorful French confections have found a passionate following in the city, with both local and international patisseries offering their unique twists. Whether you're a tourist with a sweet tooth or a local looking for the next best macaron spot, this guide will take you through the top dessert shops in Hong Kong for macarons.

Why Macarons Are a Must-Try in Hong Kong

Macarons may have originated in France, but Hong Kong has embraced them with open arms. The city’s vibrant culinary culture blends tradition with innovation, resulting in macarons that are both classic and creatively infused with local flavors. From matcha to lychee, Hong Kong’s macaron makers aren’t afraid to experiment.

The Perfect Blend of East and West

Hong Kong’s macaron scene reflects its cosmopolitan identity. French techniques meet Asian ingredients, creating a fusion that’s hard to resist. Whether you prefer traditional flavors like vanilla and chocolate or adventurous ones like black sesame or yuzu, there’s something for everyone.

Top Macaron Shops in Hong Kong

1. Pierre Hermé Hong Kong

When it comes to macarons, Pierre Hermé is a name that needs no introduction. Known as the "Picasso of Pastry," this Parisian brand has set up shop in Hong Kong, bringing its world-famous macarons to the city.

Must-Try Flavors:

  • Ispahan – A signature blend of rose, lychee, and raspberry.
  • Mogador – Passionfruit and milk chocolate for a tropical twist.

Located in ifc mall, this spot is perfect for a sweet break while shopping.

2. Ladurée Hong Kong

Another French heavyweight, Ladurée, has made its mark in Hong Kong with its pastel-colored macarons and elegant packaging. The shop’s luxurious vibe makes it a favorite for both locals and tourists.

Must-Try Flavors:

  • Salted Caramel – A perfect balance of sweet and savory.
  • Pistachio – Rich, nutty, and utterly addictive.

Find them in Harbour City for a chic dessert experience.

3. Oddies Foodies

For those who love a modern twist, Oddies Foodies is a must-visit. This local gem is famous for its "Night Wolf" dessert, but their macarons are equally impressive.

Must-Try Flavors:

  • Egg Waffle Macaron – A playful take on Hong Kong’s iconic street food.
  • Hong Kong Milk Tea – A nostalgic flavor that captures the essence of the city.

Located in Wan Chai, it’s a great stop after exploring the neighborhood’s vibrant streets.

4. SEVVA

If you’re looking for macarons with a view, SEVVA is the place to be. This rooftop lounge offers stunning skyline views along with exquisite desserts.

Must-Try Flavors:

  • Champagne & Strawberry – A luxurious pairing.
  • Earl Grey – Light, fragrant, and perfect with tea.

Head to Prince’s Building for a high-end macaron experience.

5. Bakehouse

Known for its sourdough egg tarts, Bakehouse also serves fantastic macarons. The bakery’s commitment to quality shines through in every bite.

Must-Try Flavors:

  • Honey & Lavender – A floral, soothing combination.
  • Salted Egg Yolk – A bold, local-inspired flavor.

With locations in Wan Chai and Central, it’s easily accessible for a quick treat.

Hidden Gems for Macaron Lovers

6. Sweet Dynasty

While primarily known for traditional Chinese desserts, Sweet Dynasty surprises with its macaron selection. Their versions often incorporate Asian ingredients for a unique taste.

Must-Try Flavors:

  • Red Bean – A sweet, earthy twist.
  • Mango Pomelo – Refreshing and fruity.

Find them in Tsim Sha Tsui for a fusion dessert experience.

7. The Cakery

For health-conscious macaron lovers, The Cakery offers gluten-free and organic options without compromising on taste.

Must-Try Flavors:

  • Matcha & White Chocolate – A Japanese-inspired delight.
  • Raspberry Rose – Light and aromatic.

Located in Central, it’s ideal for those with dietary restrictions.

Macaron Shopping Tips for Tourists

Best Time to Visit

Most dessert shops are busiest in the afternoon. For a more relaxed experience, visit in the morning or early evening.

Packaging for Souvenirs

Many shops, like Ladurée and Pierre Hermé, offer beautifully packaged macarons, making them perfect gifts.

Pairing Suggestions

  • With Tea – Try a floral macaron with jasmine tea.
  • With Coffee – A dark chocolate macaron pairs well with an espresso.

Final Thoughts

Hong Kong’s macaron scene is as diverse as the city itself. From high-end French patisseries to local innovators, there’s no shortage of places to satisfy your macaron cravings. Whether you’re indulging in a classic flavor or daring to try something new, these shops promise an unforgettable sweet adventure.
