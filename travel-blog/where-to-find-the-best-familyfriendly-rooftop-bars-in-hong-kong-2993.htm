Where to Find the Best Family-Friendly Rooftop Bars in Hong Kong

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong’s skyline is more than just a postcard—it’s a living, breathing spectacle of light, energy, and vertical ambition. For travelers and locals alike, experiencing the city from above isn’t just a luxury; it’s a essential part of understanding its soul. But what if you’re traveling with little ones in tow? The idea of rooftop bars might conjure images of sophisticated cocktails and late-night chatter, hardly a place for children. Yet, Hong Kong, in its endlessly surprising way, offers a growing number of rooftop venues that masterfully blend world-class views with a genuinely welcoming, family-friendly atmosphere. These are not just bars; they are urban oases where parents can unwind with a glass of wine while kids marvel at the skyscrapers, making for unforgettable family moments high above the bustling streets.

Redefining the Rooftop Experience: More Than Just Cocktails

Gone are the days when rooftops were exclusively for adults. A new wave of venues in Hong Kong understands that families want to share these incredible experiences together. These spots prioritize space, safety, and a menu that caters to all ages, offering crafted mocktails, fresh juices, and delicious sharable bites alongside their signature drinks. The ambiance is lively yet relaxed, often with music that sets a cheerful mood without overwhelming conversation. It’s about creating a shared memory where everyone, from the youngest to the oldest, feels included.

What Makes a Rooftop Bar "Family-Friendly"?

Before we explore the specific locations, it’s important to define what we’re looking for. A truly family-friendly rooftop bar in Hong Kong typically has several key features:

  • Spacious Layout: Ample room for strollers and for kids to sit comfortably without feeling cramped.
  • Engaging Views: A captivating panorama that naturally entertains children—think harbors, boats, and towering buildings.
  • Diverse Menu: A dedicated kids' menu, high-quality non-alcoholic options, and food that appeals to picky eaters.
  • Early Evening Accessibility: Operating hours that allow for pre-or-post-dinner visits, perfect for families who prefer to be home before late night.
  • Welcoming Staff: An attitude that embraces families, providing high chairs and a patient, friendly demeanor.

Top Picks for Family-Friendly Rooftop Bars in Hong Kong

Here are some of the best venues across the city that successfully combine stunning vistas with a family-welcoming vibe.

1. Sugar (Tai Ping Shan Street, Eastern District)

Perched atop the EAST Hong Kong hotel in Quarry Bay, Sugar is a legendary name in the city's rooftop scene. While it transforms into a vibrant lounge later at night, its early evening hours are perfect for families. The large, wooden-decked terrace offers breathtaking, uninterrupted views of Victoria Harbour and the Kowloon skyline. Kids will be mesmerized by the constant parade of cargo ships, ferries like the iconic Star Ferry, and airplanes coming in to land at the nearby airport. The menu features Asian-inspired tapas and pizzas that are great for sharing, along with a selection of creative mocktails. Arrive around 5:30 pm to secure a good spot and watch the city begin to light up—a magical experience for all.

2. Terrible Baby (Mong Kok, Kowloon)

Don't let the name fool you—this is one of the coolest and most surprisingly family-inclusive spots in the city. Located on the fourth floor of the Eaton HK hotel in the heart of vibrant Mong Kok, Terrible Baby is more than a bar; it's a cultural space. The rooftop terrace is adorned with lush greenery, creating a relaxed, bohemian garden feel. Adjacent to the terrace is a small, often overlooked art gallery and a music studio, which can be a curious distraction for older kids and teens. The area is safe and enclosed, and the innovative cocktail menu is matched by equally interesting zero-proof beverages. It’s a fantastic option for parents who want a dose of Hong Kong’s indie culture while keeping the kids engaged.

3. Popinjays (Central, Hong Kong Island)

For a truly special occasion, Popinjays at The Murray Hong Kong offers a luxurious yet surprisingly relaxed rooftop experience. The name refers to the local parakeets, hinting at its playful nature. The terrace provides a spectacular 360-degree view of Central’s skyscrapers and Hong Kong Park below. While it’s undoubtedly upscale, the staff are exceptionally welcoming to well-behaved children, especially during weekend brunch or early evening. The menu is a culinary adventure, and they are more than happy to prepare simpler dishes for younger guests. Watching the sunset here, with the city’s architectural wonders all around, is a memory your family will cherish. It’s a splurge, but worth it for a one-of-a-kind treat.

4. Wooloomooloo Prime (Tsim Sha Tsui, Kowloon)

Situated on the 28th floor of The One mall, Wooloomooloo Prime boasts one of the most iconic panoramic views of Hong Kong Island across Victoria Harbour. The open-air terrace is vast, allowing plenty of space for families. This is the perfect spot for that classic "I ♥ HK" photo with the skyline as your backdrop. The steakhouse menu is famous, but they also offer burgers, salads, and a kids' menu. Coming here for an early dinner as the sun sets and the Symphony of Lights show begins (at 8:00 pm) is a quintessential Hong Kong family activity. The easy access inside a major mall also means quick escapes if the weather turns or a child gets restless.

Making the Most of Your Rooftop Family Adventure

A little planning can ensure your elevated outing is smooth and enjoyable for everyone.

Timing is Everything

The golden hour for families is between 5:00 pm and 7:30 pm. You’ll avoid the late-night crowds, catch the beautiful sunset, and often benefit from "happy hour" pricing on drinks and snacks. The weather is also typically more pleasant during this time, especially in the humid summer months.

What to Pack

Even though these are sophisticated venues, coming prepared is key. * Layers: It can get windy and cooler once the sun goes down, even in summer. A light sweater or jacket for everyone is a good idea. * Compact Entertainment: While the view is the main event, a small coloring book or a quiet game on a tablet (with headphones) can help if younger kids need a brief distraction while waiting for food. * Camera: You’ll want to capture these moments. The lighting during sunset is perfect for family photos.

Navigating Reservations and Logistics

Always call ahead or check the website. While some venues are first-come, first-served, others, especially on weekends, strongly recommend reservations. When booking, clearly state you are bringing children. This allows the staff to prepare a suitable table, perhaps in a slightly more spacious corner, and have high chairs ready. Also, use apps like HKTaxi or Uber to simplify your travel to and from these locations, as navigating public transport with tired children after dark can be challenging.

Hong Kong’s family-friendly rooftop bars are a testament to the city’s dynamic and inclusive spirit. They prove that you don’t have to sacrifice style or sophistication for a great family outing. So, on your next visit to this magnificent city, make a point to rise above it all. Share a plate of fries, sip a lychee mocktail, and watch as your children’s eyes widen at the glittering urban jungle below. It’s more than just a drink with a view; it’s about sharing the awe-inspiring magic of Hong Kong with the whole family.
