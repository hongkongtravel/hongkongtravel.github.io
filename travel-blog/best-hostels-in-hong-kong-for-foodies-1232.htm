Best Hostels in Hong Kong for Foodies

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a paradise for food lovers, offering an incredible mix of Cantonese dim sum, street food, and international cuisine. For budget-conscious travelers who don’t want to miss out on the city’s culinary delights, staying at a hostel in a prime foodie location is a smart move. Here are the best hostels in Hong Kong where you can enjoy easy access to amazing eats without breaking the bank.

Top Hostels for Food Lovers

1. Yesinn @ Causeway Bay

Located in the heart of one of Hong Kong’s busiest shopping and dining districts, Yesinn @ Causeway Bay is perfect for travelers who want to explore local food scenes.

Why Foodies Love It

  • Proximity to Food Streets: Just steps away from Jardine’s Crescent, a famous street food market offering fish balls, stinky tofu, and egg waffles.
  • Affordable Eats Nearby: Plenty of dai pai dongs (open-air food stalls) and cha chaan tengs (Hong Kong-style cafes) nearby.
  • Modern Kitchenette: Some rooms come with a small kitchenette, great for reheating late-night snacks.

2. Hop Inn on Mody (Tsim Sha Tsui)

Situated in Tsim Sha Tsui, this stylish hostel is surrounded by some of the best eateries in Kowloon.

Must-Try Nearby Spots

  • Australia Dairy Company: Famous for its silky scrambled eggs and milk tea (just a 5-minute walk).
  • Temple Street Night Market: A short stroll away, offering seafood, clay pot rice, and more.
  • Local Bakeries: Multiple bakeries nearby serve fresh egg tarts and pineapple buns.

3. Mei Ho House Youth Hostel (Sham Shui Po)

A heritage building turned hostel, Mei Ho House is ideal for those who want an authentic local food experience.

Sham Shui Po’s Hidden Gems

  • Kwan Kee Bamboo Noodle: Hand-pulled noodles with rich broth (a 10-minute walk).
  • Sham Shui Po Food Market: A budget-friendly spot for roast meats, congee, and more.
  • Sweet Soup Shops: Traditional tong sui (dessert soups) like red bean and sesame paste.

Budget-Friendly Hostels Near Michelin-Starred Eats

4. Urban Pack (Central)

This lively hostel is just a short walk from Lan Kwai Fong and Soho, where you’ll find both cheap eats and high-end dining.

Foodie Perks

  • Tim Ho Wan (the cheapest Michelin-starred dim sum): Only a 15-minute MTR ride away.
  • Gai Daan Zai (egg waffles): Street vendors nearby serve this iconic snack.
  • Craft Beer Bars: Great spots to unwind after a food crawl.

5. Rainbow Lodge HK (Mong Kok)

Mong Kok is a street food heaven, and Rainbow Lodge HK puts you right in the middle of the action.

What to Eat Nearby

  • Curry Fish Balls: A must-try Hong Kong street snack.
  • Clay Pot Rice: Several famous spots within walking distance.
  • Dessert Cafés: Mango pomelo sago and durian pancakes are local favorites.

Unique Hostels with Food Experiences

6. The Mahjong (North Point)

A boutique hostel with a retro vibe, The Mahjong is close to North Point’s wet markets and hidden eateries.

Local Flavors to Explore

  • Chai Wan Kok Kee Wonton Noodles: A legendary spot for shrimp wontons.
  • Egg Tart Alley: A street famous for flaky, buttery egg tarts.
  • Traditional Tea Houses: Sip on pu-erh tea while enjoying dim sum.

7. Wontonmeen (Sheung Wan)

This artsy hostel doubles as a noodle bar, making it a dream stay for noodle lovers.

Why It Stands Out

  • In-House Wonton Noodles: Made fresh daily.
  • Sheung Wan’s Hip Cafés: Specialty coffee and fusion bites nearby.
  • Dried Seafood Street: A unique spot to explore Hong Kong’s culinary heritage.

Tips for Foodie Travelers in Hong Kong

  • Use Octopus Card: Speeds up payments at street stalls and MTR rides between food spots.
  • Follow the Locals: The longest queues often lead to the best food.
  • Try Dai Pai Dongs: These open-air food stalls offer some of the most authentic flavors.

Hong Kong’s food scene is unbeatable, and staying at these hostels ensures you’re never far from your next delicious meal. Whether you’re craving Michelin-starred dim sum or late-night street snacks, these budget-friendly stays put you right where the action is.
