Hong Kong Travel Guidelines for UK Travelers

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong remains one of the most vibrant and dynamic destinations for UK travelers, blending East-meets-West culture, towering skyscrapers, and lush green landscapes. Whether you're planning a short city break or an extended exploration, this guide covers everything from visa requirements to hidden gems.

Pre-Travel Essentials

Visa and Entry Requirements

UK passport holders can enter Hong Kong visa-free for up to 180 days, making it an incredibly accessible destination. However, always double-check the latest entry rules on the Hong Kong Immigration Department website before booking.

Health and Safety

No mandatory vaccinations are required, but it’s wise to be up-to-date on routine vaccines like Hepatitis A and Typhoid. Hong Kong has excellent healthcare, but travel insurance with medical coverage is highly recommended.

Currency and Payments

The local currency is the Hong Kong Dollar (HKD). Credit cards are widely accepted, but smaller vendors and street markets prefer cash. ATMs are plentiful, and currency exchange counters offer competitive rates.

Getting Around Hong Kong

Public Transport: Efficient and Affordable

Hong Kong’s MTR (Mass Transit Railway) is one of the world’s best subway systems, connecting major districts seamlessly. The Octopus Card (a rechargeable smart card) is a must-have for hassle-free travel on trains, buses, and even ferries.

Taxis and Ride-Hailing

Taxis are affordable but ensure the meter is running. Uber operates in Hong Kong, but local apps like HKTaxi are popular for hailing cabs.

Walking and Cycling

Central and Tsim Sha Tsui are highly walkable, while cycling is best enjoyed in areas like Tai Wai or the New Territories.

Must-Visit Attractions

Iconic Landmarks

  • Victoria Peak: Take the Peak Tram for breathtaking skyline views.
  • Star Ferry: A budget-friendly way to cross Victoria Harbour.
  • Tian Tan Buddha (Big Buddha): A spiritual and scenic day trip to Lantau Island.

Hidden Gems

  • PMQ: A creative hub in a restored police married quarters building.
  • Sai Kung: Known for its seafood and hiking trails.
  • Tai O Fishing Village: A glimpse into traditional stilt-house living.

Food and Dining

Cantonese Classics

Don’t miss dim sum, roast goose, and egg waffles (gai daan jai). For an authentic experience, try local cha chaan tengs (Hong Kong-style cafes).

Michelin-Starred Eats

Hong Kong boasts the world’s most affordable Michelin-starred meals, like Tim Ho Wan for dim sum or Kam’s Roast Goose.

Street Food Hotspots

Head to Temple Street Night Market or Mong Kok’s food stalls for fish balls, stinky tofu, and bubble tea.

Shopping and Souvenirs

Luxury and Bargains

  • Causeway Bay: High-end brands and trendy boutiques.
  • Ladies’ Market: Bargain hunting for souvenirs and fashion.
  • PMQ: Unique handmade crafts and designer goods.

Electronics and Gadgets

Sham Shui Po’s Golden Computer Centre is a tech lover’s paradise for gadgets at competitive prices.

Cultural Etiquette

Do’s and Don’ts

  • Do: Queue orderly, use both hands when giving/receiving items.
  • Don’t: Tip excessively (service charge is usually included).

Language Tips

While English is widely spoken, learning basic Cantonese phrases like “M̀h’gōi” (Thank you) can go a long way.

Seasonal Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October–December: Pleasant weather, ideal for outdoor activities.
  • January–March: Cooler temps, but Lunar New Year brings crowds.
    Avoid June–September if you dislike humidity and typhoon risks.

Festivals Worth Planning For

  • Lunar New Year: Parades, fireworks, and lion dances.
  • Mid-Autumn Festival: Lantern displays and mooncakes.
  • Hong Kong Wine & Dine Festival: A foodie’s dream in October.

Day Trips and Excursions

Macau

A 1-hour ferry ride takes you to the “Las Vegas of Asia” for Portuguese heritage and world-class casinos.

Shenzhen

Cross the border for tech shopping and theme parks like Window of the World.

Lamma Island

Escape the city for beaches, seafood, and hiking trails.

Accommodation Guide

Luxury Stays

  • The Peninsula Hong Kong: Classic colonial elegance.
  • Ritz-Carlton Hong Kong: Sky-high luxury with stunning views.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Mingle Hostel: Social and centrally located.
  • Hop Inn: Stylish and affordable in Mong Kok.

Unique Stays

  • Tai O Heritage Hotel: A converted police station with vintage charm.
  • The Pottinger: Boutique luxury in Central.

Connectivity and SIM Cards

Staying Connected

Purchase a tourist SIM card at the airport or convenience stores. Major providers include CSL, 3HK, and China Mobile. Free Wi-Fi is available in many public areas.

Sustainable Travel Tips

Eco-Friendly Choices

  • Use reusable bottles (tap water is safe but often boiled).
  • Support local markets and eco-tours like HK Farm.
  • Opt for electric buses and ferries when possible.

Hong Kong’s energy is infectious, and with these tips, UK travelers can dive into its bustling streets, serene hikes, and culinary delights with confidence. Whether it’s your first visit or a return trip, the city always has something new to discover.
