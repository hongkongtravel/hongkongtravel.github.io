Best Halal Restaurants in Hong Kong for 2024

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a vibrant melting pot of cultures, and its culinary scene reflects this diversity. For Muslim travelers or locals seeking Halal-certified dining options, the city offers an impressive array of choices—from traditional Cantonese dishes to Middle Eastern flavors and South Asian delights. Whether you're craving dim sum, kebabs, or spicy curries, Hong Kong’s Halal restaurants won’t disappoint. Here’s a curated list of the best Halal restaurants in Hong Kong for 2024, ensuring you enjoy delicious meals without compromising dietary requirements.

Top Halal-Friendly Eateries in Hong Kong

1. Islamic Centre Canteen (Wan Chai)

A hidden gem near the Wan Chai Mosque, this no-frills canteen serves authentic Halal Cantonese cuisine. Favorites include beef brisket noodles, steamed dumplings, and their famous curry fish balls. The prices are budget-friendly, making it a great spot for a quick yet satisfying meal.

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Beef Brisket Noodles – Tender beef in a rich broth with perfectly cooked noodles.
  • Curry Fish Balls – A Hong Kong street food staple with a Halal twist.

2. Mala Islamic Restaurant (Tsim Sha Tsui)

For those who love spicy food, Mala Islamic Restaurant is a must-visit. Specializing in Sichuan and Xinjiang flavors, this eatery offers bold, aromatic dishes that pack a punch.

Signature Picks:

  • Lamb Skewers – Marinated with cumin and chili for an unforgettable taste.
  • Spicy Beef Hot Pot – A fiery broth loaded with tender beef slices and fresh vegetables.

3. Delhi Club (Central)

A long-standing favorite for North Indian cuisine, Delhi Club serves up rich curries, fluffy naans, and perfectly grilled kebabs. The cozy ambiance makes it ideal for both casual lunches and intimate dinners.

Highlights:

  • Butter Chicken – Creamy, flavorful, and utterly addictive.
  • Garlic Naan – Freshly baked and perfect for scooping up curry.

Best Halal Dim Sum in Hong Kong

4. Islamic Restaurant (Tsim Sha Tsui & Causeway Bay)

One of the few places offering Halal-certified dim sum, Islamic Restaurant is a game-changer for Muslim foodies. Their steamed buns, dumplings, and rice rolls are prepared with high-quality Halal ingredients.

Dim Sum Favorites:

  • Halal Siu Mai – Juicy pork-free dumplings with a savory filling.
  • Beef Rice Rolls – Silky smooth rice noodle rolls stuffed with seasoned beef.

5. The Chinese Muslim Restaurant (Jordan)

Another excellent option for Halal dim sum, this restaurant is known for its hearty portions and traditional flavors. The beef dumplings and sesame pancakes are crowd-pleasers.

Middle Eastern & International Halal Cuisine

6. Habibi (Lan Kwai Fong)

If you're in the mood for Middle Eastern fare, Habibi delivers with its authentic Lebanese dishes. The mezze platter, hummus, and shawarma are all prepared with Halal-certified meats.

Recommended Dishes:

  • Mixed Grill Platter – A carnivore’s dream with lamb chops, chicken, and kebabs.
  • Baklava – A sweet, flaky dessert to end your meal on a high note.

7. Mum’s Not Home (Sheung Wan)

A quirky café with a Halal-friendly menu, Mum’s Not Home offers fusion dishes like nasi lemak burgers and matcha desserts. The artsy vibe makes it a great spot for Instagram-worthy shots.

Halal Street Food & Quick Bites

8. Wai Kee Noodle Cafe (Sham Shui Po)

Famous for its Halal beef brisket noodles, this humble eatery is a local favorite. The broth is simmered for hours, resulting in a deeply flavorful experience.

9. Kebab House (Mong Kok)

For a quick, satisfying meal, Kebab House serves juicy lamb and chicken wraps with fresh veggies and tangy sauces. Perfect for on-the-go dining.

Upscale Halal Dining Experiences

10. Ebeneezer’s Kebabs & Pizzeria (Multiple Locations)

While known for its kebabs, Ebeneezer’s also offers Halal-certified pizzas and burgers. The cheese naan is a must-try!

11. The Salted Pig (Central)

A Western-style Halal restaurant offering gourmet burgers, steaks, and ribs. The relaxed yet upscale setting makes it great for group dinners.

Final Tips for Halal Dining in Hong Kong

  • Check Certifications – Some restaurants may serve Halal food but lack official certification, so always verify.
  • Peak Hours – Popular spots like Islamic Centre Canteen can get crowded during lunch, so plan accordingly.
  • Vegetarian Options – Many Halal restaurants also offer excellent plant-based dishes for those who prefer meat-free meals.

Hong Kong’s Halal dining scene continues to grow, blending local flavors with international influences. Whether you're a tourist or a resident, these restaurants promise unforgettable culinary adventures in 2024.
