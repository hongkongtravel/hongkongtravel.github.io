Hong Kong’s Best Scenic Ferry Rides

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a city of contrasts, where towering skyscrapers meet serene harbors, and bustling streets give way to tranquil islands. One of the best ways to experience this dynamic metropolis is by hopping aboard one of its iconic ferry rides. From the legendary Star Ferry to lesser-known gems, these journeys offer breathtaking views, rich history, and a unique perspective of Hong Kong’s vibrant waterfront.

The Star Ferry: A Timeless Classic

No visit to Hong Kong is complete without a ride on the Star Ferry. This century-old service shuttles passengers between Central and Tsim Sha Tsui, offering unparalleled views of Victoria Harbour. The short 10-minute trip feels like a journey through time, with the ferry’s wooden benches and vintage charm transporting you to old Hong Kong.

Why It’s a Must-Do

  • Panoramic Skyline Views: Capture postcard-perfect shots of Hong Kong’s skyline, especially stunning at sunset or during the nightly Symphony of Lights show.
  • Affordable Luxury: For just a few HKD, you get a front-row seat to one of the world’s most famous harbors.
  • Historical Significance: The Star Ferry has operated since 1888 and remains a symbol of Hong Kong’s enduring spirit.

Pro Tip: Sit on the upper deck for the best views and fewer crowds.

Lamma Island Ferry: A Escape to Nature

For a break from the urban hustle, the Lamma Island ferry is a perfect choice. Departing from Central Pier 4, this 30-minute ride takes you to the laid-back island of Lamma, known for its seafood, hiking trails, and bohemian vibe.

Highlights of the Ride

  • Scenic Coastline: Spot fishing villages, rocky shores, and lush hillsides as you approach the island.
  • Car-Free Bliss: Lamma Island bans cars, making it a peaceful retreat for nature lovers.
  • Seafood Feasts: Disembark at Yung Shue Wan or Sok Kwu Wan for fresh seafood at waterfront restaurants.

Fun Fact: Lamma Island is home to Hong Kong’s first wind turbine, visible from the ferry.

Cheung Chau Ferry: A Taste of Island Life

Another gem in Hong Kong’s ferry network is the route to Cheung Chau, a small island with a big personality. Known for its annual Bun Festival and sandy beaches, Cheung Chau feels worlds away from the city.

What Makes It Special

  • Quaint Village Vibes: Wander narrow alleys lined with traditional shops and temples.
  • Beachside Relaxation: Head to Tung Wan Beach for a swim or a seaside picnic.
  • Cultural Festivals: If visiting in spring, don’t miss the Bun Festival’s thrilling bun-scrambling competition.

Pro Tip: Rent a bike to explore the island’s hidden corners.

Peng Chau Ferry: A Hidden Gem

For those seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure, the Peng Chau ferry is a delightful surprise. This tiny island offers a glimpse into Hong Kong’s slower-paced, community-driven lifestyle.

Why Peng Chau Stands Out

  • Artistic Flair: Discover indie galleries and quirky cafes tucked away in the village.
  • Finger Hill Hike: A short but rewarding hike leads to panoramic views of the surrounding islands.
  • Local Eats: Try the island’s famous pineapple buns or fresh seafood at family-run eateries.

The Outlying Islands Helicopter Ferry: A Luxurious Alternative

For a splurge-worthy experience, consider the Helicopter Ferry to Macau or Shenzhen. While not a traditional ferry, these short flights offer jaw-dropping aerial views of Hong Kong’s coastline and skyscrapers.

Why Choose the Helicopter Ferry?

  • Unmatched Views: Soar above Victoria Harbour and see Hong Kong from a bird’s-eye perspective.
  • Time-Saving: Reach Macau in just 15 minutes, perfect for a day trip.
  • Bucket-List Experience: A thrilling way to cap off your Hong Kong adventure.

Tips for Enjoying Hong Kong’s Ferry Rides

To make the most of your ferry journeys, keep these tips in mind:
- Octopus Card Convenience: Use your Octopus card for seamless payment on most ferries.
- Timing Matters: Avoid rush hours (8–9 AM and 6–7 PM) for a more relaxed ride.
- Weather Watch: Clear days offer the best views, but even foggy rides have a mystical charm.

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a longtime resident, Hong Kong’s ferry rides promise unforgettable moments on the water. Each route tells a different story, blending history, culture, and natural beauty into one extraordinary experience.
