How to Visit Hong Kong’s Victoria Peak with Kids

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Victoria Peak, or simply "The Peak," is one of Hong Kong’s most iconic attractions. With breathtaking skyline views, lush hiking trails, and family-friendly activities, it’s a must-visit for families traveling with kids. Here’s your ultimate guide to making the most of your trip to Victoria Peak with little ones in tow.

Why Victoria Peak is Perfect for Families

Victoria Peak isn’t just about the view—though the panoramic vista of Hong Kong’s skyline is reason enough to visit. The Peak offers a mix of nature, entertainment, and convenience, making it ideal for families. Kids will love the tram ride, interactive exhibits, and open spaces to run around, while parents can enjoy the scenery and dining options.

The Peak Tram: A Kid-Friendly Adventure

No visit to Victoria Peak is complete without riding the historic Peak Tram. This funicular railway has been operating since 1888 and climbs steeply up the mountainside, offering thrilling views along the way.

Tips for Riding the Peak Tram with Kids:

  • Arrive early to avoid long queues, especially on weekends.
  • Grab seats on the right side for the best views during the ascent.
  • Hold onto younger kids—the steep incline can be surprising!
  • Consider the Peak Tram Sky Pass, which includes entry to the Sky Terrace 428 for unobstructed views.

Top Family-Friendly Activities at Victoria Peak

Once you reach the top, there’s plenty to keep kids entertained. Here are the best activities to enjoy as a family.

1. Sky Terrace 428: Hong Kong’s Highest Viewing Platform

At 428 meters above sea level, Sky Terrace 428 offers 360-degree views of Hong Kong’s skyline, Victoria Harbour, and the surrounding islands. Kids will love spotting landmarks like the ICC Tower and Victoria Harbour from this vantage point.

Pro Tip: Visit just before sunset to see the city transition from day to night—the light show over the harbor is magical.

2. Madame Tussauds Hong Kong

Located at The Peak Tower, Madame Tussauds is a hit with kids who love posing with lifelike wax figures of celebrities, superheroes, and historical figures. Interactive exhibits, like the Marvel 4D Experience, add extra fun.

Best for: Kids aged 5+ who enjoy photo ops and pop culture.

3. The Peak Circle Walk: A Stroller-Friendly Hike

If your family enjoys light hikes, the Peak Circle Walk is a paved, stroller-friendly trail that loops around the summit. The 3.5 km walk offers shaded paths, scenic viewpoints, and even a playground along the way.

Kid-Friendly Stops:
- Lugard Road Lookout – A great spot for family photos.
- Harlech Road Playground – Let little ones burn off energy.

4. Peak Galleria: Shopping and Dining

The Peak Galleria is a shopping and dining hub with family-friendly restaurants and souvenir shops.

Best Kid-Friendly Eateries:

  • Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. – Fun, themed dining with American favorites.
  • Starbucks – A reliable spot for snacks and baby-changing facilities.

Practical Tips for Visiting Victoria Peak with Kids

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable visit, keep these tips in mind.

Best Time to Visit

  • Weekday mornings are less crowded.
  • Avoid weekends and public holidays if possible.

What to Bring

  • Light jackets—it can be windy at the top.
  • Snacks and water—though there are food options, having backups helps.
  • A baby carrier if you have toddlers (some paths are stroller-friendly, but not all).

Getting There

  • Peak Tram (most scenic option).
  • Bus 15 from Central (budget-friendly and less crowded).
  • Taxi (convenient but pricier).

Final Thoughts

Victoria Peak is a fantastic destination for families, combining adventure, education, and relaxation. Whether you’re riding the tram, exploring Madame Tussauds, or simply soaking in the views, there’s something for everyone. With a little planning, your family trip to The Peak will be unforgettable.
