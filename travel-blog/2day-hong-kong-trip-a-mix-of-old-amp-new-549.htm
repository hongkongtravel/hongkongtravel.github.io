2-Day Hong Kong Trip: A Mix of Old & New

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a city where tradition and modernity collide in the most exhilarating way. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, a well-planned 2-day itinerary can give you a taste of both worlds—historic neighborhoods, cutting-edge skyscrapers, and everything in between. Here’s how to make the most of 48 hours in this dynamic metropolis.

Day 1: Exploring Hong Kong’s Historic Roots

Morning: Temple Street & Kowloon’s Hidden Gems

Start your day in Jordan, where the famous Temple Street Night Market may be quiet in the morning, but the surrounding area buzzes with local life. Grab breakfast at a cha chaan teng (Hong Kong-style café)—order a pineapple bun (bo lo bao) and a cup of milk tea for an authentic start.

Next, head to Tin Hau Temple in Yau Ma Tei, a serene escape from the urban rush. Nearby, the Yau Ma Tei Theatre and Fruit Market offer glimpses into old Kowloon’s cultural heritage.

Afternoon: Sham Shui Po’s Street Markets

Hop on the MTR to Sham Shui Po, a district packed with electronics, fabric, and vintage shops. Don’t miss:
- Golden Computer Centre for tech bargains
- Ki Lung Street Market for quirky souvenirs
- Kwan Kee Store for traditional bamboo noodle bowls

For lunch, try cheung fun (rice noodle rolls) at Hop Yik Tai, a Michelin-recommended street stall.

Evening: Victoria Harbour & Symphony of Lights

As dusk falls, take the Star Ferry from Tsim Sha Tsui to Central—the best $3 you’ll spend in Hong Kong. The skyline views are unbeatable.

Back in Tsim Sha Tsui, stroll the Avenue of Stars and catch the Symphony of Lights show at 8 PM. Cap the night with cocktails at Ozone Bar (Ritz-Carlton’s 118th-floor sky bar) or a casual dai pai dong (open-air food stall) meal in Mong Kok.

Day 2: Modern Hong Kong & Scenic Escapes

Morning: The Peak & Hong Kong’s Skyline

Rise early and take the Peak Tram to Victoria Peak. The morning mist often clears by 9 AM, revealing panoramic views of the city. Walk the Peak Circle Trail for lush greenery and Instagram-worthy angles.

Descend to Central for brunch at Lan Fong Yuen, home of the original pantyhose milk tea. Then explore:
- PMQ, a creative hub in a former police dormitory
- Tai Kwun, a restored colonial-era prison turned arts center

Afternoon: Arts & Island Vibes

Take a ferry to Lamma Island for a laid-back contrast to the urban buzz. Highlights:
- Hung Shing Yeh Beach
- Seafood lunch at Rainbow Restaurant
- The Lamma Island Family Trail with coastal views

Alternatively, art lovers can visit M+ Museum in West Kowloon or the Hong Kong Palace Museum for contemporary and imperial Chinese exhibits.

Evening: Neon Nights & Rooftop Revelry

Return to the city for a sunset drink at Sevva (25/F, Prince’s Building) overlooking Statue Square. Then dive into:
- Lan Kwai Fong for bar-hopping
- Duddell Street for speakeasy vibes
- Tsim Chai Kee for wonton noodles if you need a midnight snack

Pro Tips for Your Trip

  • Octopus Card: Essential for MTR, buses, and even convenience stores.
  • Weather: Check forecasts—Hong Kong’s humidity can be intense.
  • Language: English is widely spoken, but learning basic Cantonese phrases (e.g., “m̀h’gōi” for thank you) earns smiles.

Hong Kong’s magic lies in its contrasts. From incense-filled temples to laser-lit skyscrapers, this 2-day adventure proves you don’t need to choose between old and new—you can have both.
