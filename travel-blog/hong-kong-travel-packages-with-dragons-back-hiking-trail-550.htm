Hong Kong Travel Packages with Dragon’s Back Hiking Trail

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a dazzling metropolis where skyscrapers meet lush mountains, offering travelers a unique blend of urban excitement and natural beauty. Among its many attractions, the Dragon’s Back Hiking Trail stands out as a must-visit for adventure seekers and nature lovers. This iconic trail, named for its undulating ridge resembling a dragon’s spine, provides breathtaking views of the South China Sea, Shek O Beach, and the surrounding islands.

If you're planning a trip to Hong Kong, consider booking a travel package that includes the Dragon’s Back Trail—it’s the perfect way to experience the city’s dynamic contrasts. Below, we’ll explore why this hike is a highlight, the best travel packages available, and tips to make the most of your adventure.

Why the Dragon’s Back Trail is a Must-Do

Stunning Scenery and Accessibility

Unlike many challenging hikes, the Dragon’s Back Trail is relatively moderate, making it accessible to both beginners and experienced hikers. The 8.5-kilometer route takes about 2-3 hours to complete, winding through dense forests, open ridges, and ending near the picturesque Shek O Beach. The panoramic views from the summit are unparalleled, offering a perfect Instagram-worthy moment.

A Break from the City Bustle

Hong Kong is famous for its fast-paced lifestyle, but the Dragon’s Back Trail provides a serene escape. Just a 30-minute MTR and bus ride from Central, this trail transports you into a world of tranquility. The contrast between the urban jungle and the natural landscape is one of Hong Kong’s most captivating features.

Featured in Global Rankings

The trail gained international fame after being named "Asia’s Best Urban Hike" by Time Magazine. Its reputation has only grown, attracting hikers from around the world. Including it in your Hong Kong itinerary ensures you’re experiencing one of the region’s top outdoor adventures.

Best Hong Kong Travel Packages Featuring Dragon’s Back

Several tour operators offer customized Hong Kong travel packages that include the Dragon’s Back Trail. Here are some top options:

1. The Classic Hong Kong Explorer

This package combines city sightseeing with outdoor adventures. Highlights include:
- Guided hike on the Dragon’s Back Trail
- Victoria Peak tram ride
- Star Ferry cruise
- Street food tour in Mong Kok

Perfect for travelers who want a balanced mix of urban and natural experiences.

2. Luxury Escape with a Hike

For those who prefer comfort without sacrificing adventure, this premium package includes:
- Private guided Dragon’s Back hike with a gourmet picnic
- Stay at a 5-star hotel (The Peninsula or Four Seasons)
- Sunset junk boat cruise
- Michelin-starred dining experience

Ideal for couples or travelers seeking a high-end experience.

3. Budget-Friendly Backpacker’s Special

If you’re traveling on a shoestring, this package offers:
- Self-guided Dragon’s Back hike with a map and tips
- Stay in a trendy hostel in Causeway Bay
- Local dim sum breakfast
- Free walking tour of Central

Great for solo travelers or groups looking for affordability without missing key experiences.

Essential Tips for Hiking Dragon’s Back

Best Time to Go

The trail is open year-round, but the best seasons are autumn (October–December) and spring (March–April) when temperatures are mild. Avoid summer due to high humidity and occasional typhoons.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable hiking shoes (the trail can be slippery)
  • Sunscreen and a hat (parts of the trail are exposed)
  • Plenty of water (no shops along the way)
  • Camera or smartphone (you’ll want to capture the views)

How to Get There

From Sai Wan Ho MTR Station, take bus #9 to To Tei Wan Village, the trail’s starting point. Alternatively, taxis are affordable and convenient.

Beyond the Hike: Nearby Attractions

After conquering the Dragon’s Back, extend your adventure with these nearby spots:

Shek O Beach

A short walk from the trail’s end, this sandy beach is perfect for a post-hike swim. The laid-back village has charming seafood restaurants where you can refuel.

Big Wave Bay

Another great beach nearby, popular with surfers. Rent a board or just relax with a coconut in hand.

Stanley Market

A 20-minute bus ride away, this bustling market is ideal for souvenirs, local crafts, and waterfront dining.

Final Thoughts

A Hong Kong travel package with the Dragon’s Back Trail is more than just a hike—it’s an unforgettable journey through the city’s diverse landscapes. Whether you opt for luxury or budget-friendly options, this trail will leave you with memories of Hong Kong’s wilder, more serene side.

So pack your hiking gear, book your package, and get ready to explore one of Asia’s most breathtaking trails!
