The Best Day Trips from Hong Kong

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a vibrant metropolis with endless attractions, but sometimes you need a break from the skyscrapers and bustling streets. Luckily, the city is surrounded by incredible destinations perfect for day trips—whether you're craving nature, culture, or adventure. Here are some of the best getaways just a short journey from Hong Kong.

Macau: The Las Vegas of Asia

Why Visit Macau?

Just an hour away by ferry, Macau offers a fascinating blend of Portuguese heritage and modern luxury. Known for its glamorous casinos, this Special Administrative Region also boasts UNESCO-listed historic sites and mouthwatering cuisine.

Top Attractions

  • The Ruins of St. Paul’s – The iconic facade of this 17th-century church is a must-see.
  • Senado Square – A charming European-style plaza with pastel-colored buildings.
  • The Venetian Macao – A massive resort with gondola rides and high-end shopping.
  • A-Ma Temple – One of Macau’s oldest Taoist temples.

Food to Try

Don’t leave without sampling Portuguese egg tarts (pastéis de nata) or African chicken, a Macanese specialty.

Lantau Island: Nature & Culture

Why Visit Lantau?

Lantau is Hong Kong’s largest island, offering lush landscapes, serene beaches, and cultural landmarks like the Tian Tan Buddha.

Must-Do Activities

  • Ngong Ping 360 Cable Car – Stunning views of the mountains and sea.
  • Po Lin Monastery & Big Buddha – A spiritual retreat with a giant bronze Buddha statue.
  • Tai O Fishing Village – A stilt-house village known for its salted fish and boat tours.
  • Cheung Sha Beach – One of Hong Kong’s longest and most peaceful beaches.

Hiking Trails

For adventure seekers, the Lantau Trail offers breathtaking hikes, including the challenging Sunset Peak.

Shenzhen: Shopping & Tech Paradise

Why Visit Shenzhen?

Just across the border, Shenzhen is a futuristic city with cutting-edge technology, massive malls, and theme parks.

Best Places to Explore

  • Dafen Oil Painting Village – Where you can buy affordable hand-painted artworks.
  • OCT Loft Creative Culture Park – A hipster hub with galleries and cafes.
  • Window of the World – A theme park featuring mini replicas of global landmarks.
  • Huaqiangbei Electronics Market – A tech lover’s dream for gadgets and components.

Food Highlights

Try Chao Zhou porridge or Sichuan hot pot for an authentic taste of mainland China.

Sai Kung: Hong Kong’s Hidden Gem

Why Visit Sai Kung?

Known as the "back garden of Hong Kong," Sai Kung is famous for its seafood, hiking trails, and pristine beaches.

Things to Do

  • Sai Kung Pier – Hop on a boat to nearby islands like Sharp Island or Tai Long Wan.
  • Geopark Tours – Explore the UNESCO-listed Hong Kong Global Geopark with its unique rock formations.
  • Hiking in Sai Kung East Country Park – Trails like MacLehose Trail Section 2 offer coastal views.
  • Seafood Feast – Dine at waterfront restaurants for fresh catches like lobster and crab.

Cheung Chau: A Charming Island Escape

Why Visit Cheung Chau?

This car-free island is perfect for a laid-back day trip with its narrow streets, temples, and beaches.

Highlights

  • Pak Tai Temple – A historic temple dedicated to the Taoist sea god.
  • Cheung Po Tsai Cave – A pirate hideout with a bit of local folklore.
  • Tung Wan Beach – A great spot for swimming and relaxing.
  • Bun Festival (May) – If you visit during this festival, you’ll see the famous bun-scrambling competition.

Local Eats

Try fish balls and mango mochi from street vendors.

Guangzhou: A Taste of Cantonese Culture

Why Visit Guangzhou?

As the capital of Guangdong Province, Guangzhou is rich in history, food, and commerce.

Key Attractions

  • Canton Tower – A futuristic landmark with an observation deck.
  • Chen Clan Ancestral Hall – A masterpiece of Lingnan architecture.
  • Shamian Island – A colonial-era district with European-style buildings.
  • Yuexiu Park – Home to the iconic Five Rams Statue.

Must-Try Dishes

Indulge in dim sum, roast goose, and double-skin milk pudding.

Tai Mo Shan: Hong Kong’s Highest Peak

Why Visit Tai Mo Shan?

For those who love hiking and panoramic views, Tai Mo Shan is the perfect day trip.

What to Expect

  • Hiking Trails – The MacLehose Trail starts here, offering varying difficulty levels.
  • Waterfall Walk – Visit the Ng Tung Chai Waterfalls for a refreshing trek.
  • Cloudy Views – The peak is often shrouded in mist, creating a mystical atmosphere.

Best Time to Go

Early morning hikes provide cooler temperatures and clearer skies.

Zhuhai: Coastal Relaxation

Why Visit Zhuhai?

This seaside city is known for its clean beaches, resorts, and proximity to Macau.

Top Spots

  • Lover’s Road – A scenic coastal pathway.
  • Chimelong Ocean Kingdom – One of the world’s largest aquariums.
  • New Yuan Ming Palace – A replica of Beijing’s Old Summer Palace.

Food Recommendations

Seafood lovers should try steamed oysters and Zhuhai-style grilled squid.

Final Thoughts

Each of these destinations offers something unique, whether it’s history, nature, or culinary delights. The best part? They’re all easily accessible from Hong Kong, making them perfect for a quick escape. So pack your bag, grab your camera, and get ready to explore!
