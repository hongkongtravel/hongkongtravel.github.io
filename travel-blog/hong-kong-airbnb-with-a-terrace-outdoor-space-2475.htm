Hong Kong Airbnb with a Terrace: Outdoor Space

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong is a city of contrasts—skyscrapers meet lush mountains, bustling streets hide serene temples, and tiny apartments often come with surprisingly luxurious outdoor spaces. If you’re planning a trip to this vibrant metropolis, staying in an Airbnb with a terrace can elevate your experience from ordinary to unforgettable.

Why Choose an Airbnb with a Terrace in Hong Kong?

Hong Kong’s compact living spaces are legendary, but that doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice fresh air and open skies. A terrace offers a private retreat where you can unwind after a day of exploring, enjoy morning coffee with a view, or even host a small gathering under the stars.

The Best Neighborhoods for Terrace Stays

Not all areas in Hong Kong are created equal when it comes to outdoor space. Here are some top picks:

1. Sheung Wan

This trendy district blends old-world charm with modern cafes and boutique shops. Many Airbnbs here feature rooftops with stunning harbor views.

2. Sai Kung

For a more laid-back vibe, Sai Kung offers terraces surrounded by greenery and even ocean glimpses. Perfect for nature lovers.

3. Mid-Levels

Perched on the hillside, Mid-Levels provides sweeping city views. Terraces here often feel like hidden oases above the urban hustle.

4. Repulse Bay

If beachside relaxation is your priority, Repulse Bay’s terraces let you enjoy the sea breeze without leaving your accommodation.

What to Look for in a Terrace Airbnb

Not all terraces are created equal. Here’s what to consider before booking:

Size and Privacy

Some terraces are cozy nooks just big enough for two, while others can accommodate a small group. Check photos carefully to ensure it meets your needs.

Furniture and Amenities

A well-equipped terrace might have lounge chairs, a dining set, or even a BBQ grill. If you plan to spend time outdoors, these extras make a big difference.

View

Hong Kong’s skyline is iconic. Whether you prefer harbor lights, mountain greenery, or a mix of both, prioritize listings that highlight their vistas.

Accessibility

Some terraces require climbing narrow stairs or are shared with other guests. If convenience is key, opt for a private, easily accessible space.

Unique Terrace Experiences in Hong Kong

Beyond just a place to stay, a terrace can enhance your trip in unexpected ways:

Sunrise Yoga with a View

Start your day with a calming session as the city wakes up. The combination of fresh air and panoramic views is unbeatable.

Alfresco Dining

Pick up local delicacies from nearby markets and enjoy a feast under the stars. Many terraces have tables perfect for a romantic dinner or family meal.

Stargazing in the City

While Hong Kong is known for its light pollution, some higher-altitude terraces offer surprisingly clear night skies.

Top Airbnb Listings with Terraces

Here are a few standout options to inspire your search:

1. The Sky Garden Retreat (Sheung Wan)

This spacious terrace features a lush garden, lounge beds, and a direct view of Victoria Harbour. Ideal for couples or solo travelers seeking tranquility.

2. The Hillside Escape (Mid-Levels)

A modern minimalist design with a terrace that overlooks the city’s glittering lights. Comes with a high-end sound system for evening chill sessions.

3. The Beachside Terrace (Repulse Bay)

Steps from the sand, this Airbnb offers a breezy terrace with a hammock and outdoor shower—perfect for a tropical getaway vibe.

Tips for Booking Your Perfect Terrace Stay

  • Book Early: Prime terrace listings go fast, especially during peak seasons like autumn and spring.
  • Read Reviews: Past guests often mention noise levels, cleanliness, and whether the terrace matches the photos.
  • Check House Rules: Some hosts have restrictions on terrace use, like quiet hours or no smoking.

Final Thoughts

A terrace in Hong Kong isn’t just an add-on—it’s a game-changer. Whether you’re sipping cocktails at sunset or meditating to the hum of the city below, these outdoor spaces offer a rare luxury in one of the world’s most densely populated cities. So why settle for a standard hotel room when you can have your own slice of sky?
