Hong Kong’s Travel Guidelines for Singaporeans in 2025

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Hong Kong remains one of the most vibrant and dynamic travel destinations for Singaporeans, blending East-meets-West culture, stunning skyline views, and world-class cuisine. As we step into 2025, several updates to travel guidelines, attractions, and local trends make this an exciting time to plan your trip. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a frequent traveler, here’s everything you need to know before booking your flight.

Updated Entry Requirements for Singaporeans

Visa-Free Travel Continues

Good news for Singaporean passport holders: Hong Kong still offers visa-free entry for stays of up to 90 days. However, ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your planned departure date.

Digital Arrival Cards Replace Paper Forms

Hong Kong has fully transitioned to a digital arrival card system in 2025. Singaporeans must complete the online form 48 hours before arrival via the official Immigration Department website or mobile app. A QR code will be generated for smooth immigration clearance.

Health and Safety Protocols

While most pandemic-era restrictions have been lifted, Hong Kong recommends:
- Vaccination: No mandatory COVID-19 vaccines, but check for updates on seasonal flu or other health advisories.
- Travel Insurance: Highly recommended for medical coverage, as public healthcare for tourists is limited.

Must-Visit Attractions in 2025

The Reimagined Victoria Harbour

Hong Kong’s iconic waterfront has undergone a major revamp, with new floating piers, extended promenades, and nighttime drone shows replacing the traditional Symphony of Lights. Don’t miss the Harbour Chill Carnival, a monthly event featuring live music and pop-up markets.

Sky100’s Augmented Reality Experience

The observation deck at the International Commerce Centre (ICC) now offers AR-powered tours, allowing visitors to "see" Hong Kong’s history unfold through their smartphone screens. Perfect for history buffs and tech enthusiasts alike.

Lantau Island’s Hidden Gems

Beyond Disneyland and the Big Buddha, Lantau’s Tai O Fishing Village has gained popularity for its stilt houses and dolphin-watching tours. New eco-trails have also opened, offering breathtaking hikes with minimal crowds.

Food and Dining Trends

The Rise of "Cha Chaan Teng 2.0"

Hong Kong’s classic diners (cha chaan teng) are getting a modern twist, with chefs reinventing comfort food like pineapple buns with matcha fillings and silk-stock milk tea soft-serve. Must-try spots include Kam Wah Café and Australia Dairy Company’s new fusion menu.

Michelin-Starred Street Food

The 2025 Michelin Guide has added three new street food stalls to its list, including a soy sauce-braised squid stand in Mong Kok and a vegan egg waffle vendor in Sham Shui Po. Affordable luxury at its finest!

Shopping and Souvenirs

Sustainable Fashion in PMQ

The historic PMQ creative hub in Central is now a hotspot for sustainable fashion. Look out for local brands like The R Collective and Unspun, which offer upcycled clothing and custom-fit jeans made from recycled denim.

Tech Gadgets at Golden Computer Arcade

Singaporeans love a good tech haul, and Hong Kong’s Golden Computer Arcade remains the go-to for cutting-edge gadgets. In 2025, expect AI-powered travel accessories and limited-edition gaming peripherals at competitive prices.

Transportation Tips

Octopus Card Goes Fully Digital

The beloved Octopus card can now be added to your Apple Wallet or Google Pay, eliminating the need for a physical card. Simply tap your phone at MTR gates, buses, and even convenience stores.

New Cross-Border High-Speed Rail Links

The Guangzhou-Shenzhen-Hong Kong Express Rail Link has expanded, making day trips to Shenzhen’s tech districts or Guangzhou’s cultural sites easier than ever. Pro tip: Book tickets online to avoid queues.

Cultural Etiquette Reminders

Tipping Practices

Unlike Singapore, tipping in Hong Kong is not mandatory but appreciated in upscale restaurants (5-10%) and for exceptional service. Taxi drivers usually round up fares.

Language Tips

While English is widely spoken, learning a few Cantonese phrases like "M̀h’gōi" (Thank you) or "Néih hóu" (Hello) can go a long way in local markets.

Seasonal Events to Plan Around

March: Art Basel Hong Kong

Asia’s premier art fair returns with larger-than-ever installations and pop-up galleries. Secure tickets early—this event draws global crowds.

October: Hong Kong Wine & Dine Festival

A paradise for foodies, this festival now features AI-curated wine pairings and masterclasses by celebrity chefs.

December: WinterFest

Hong Kong’s festive season is magical, with floating Christmas markets in Victoria Harbour and New Year’s Eve fireworks synchronized to a digital light show.

Final Practical Tips

  • Currency: Carry some HKD cash for small vendors, though card payments are widely accepted.
  • Weather: Pack layers—Hong Kong’s winters (Dec-Feb) can be chilly, while summers (Jun-Sep) are humid and rainy.
  • Connectivity: Pre-order a 4G/5G SIM card at the airport or use eSIMs for seamless data access.

Hong Kong in 2025 promises an unforgettable blend of tradition and innovation. With these guidelines in hand, Singaporean travelers can explore the city with confidence and ease. Happy travels!
